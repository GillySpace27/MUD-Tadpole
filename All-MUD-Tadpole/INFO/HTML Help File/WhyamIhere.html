How do I measure this complex pulse?

So, you've got yourself an ultrafast laser, and you want to know what the pulses you're making look like. Well, for short pulses, GRENOULLIE is an amazing technique that can tell us the spectrum and spectral phase of the pulse, which we can then Fourier transform to look at it in time.


MUD tadpole utilizes this technique, extending it to enable the measurement of much more complicated pulses. First, a reference pulse is measured by a GRENOULLIE. We then interfere this known pulse with a much longer unknown pulse. By translating the reference pulse across the unknown pulse, we can take many cross sections that tell us the spectral phase difference between the reference pulse and the unknown pulse at that portion of the unknown pulse. Each cross section reveals a piece of the pulse, and they can then be concatenated to reveal the full nature of the pulse.


But we're getting ahead of ourselves. Lets take a look at the base case, called SEA TADPOLE, and then we can move on to the more complicated MUD version.
